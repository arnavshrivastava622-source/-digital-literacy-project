STAY SAFE ONLINE — PREVENTION CHECKLIST
CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Task 5
VIT Bhopal University

=====================================================
🔐 GENERAL SAFETY
=====================================================

1. Never click on links in unknown or suspicious 
   emails or SMS messages.

2. Always check the sender's email address carefully 
   before responding or clicking anything.

3. Use strong passwords with uppercase letters, 
   numbers, and special characters.

4. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all 
   your important accounts like email and GitHub.

5. Never share your OTP with anyone — not even 
   people claiming to be bank officials or college 
   administration.

=====================================================
💰 FINANCIAL / UPI SAFETY
=====================================================

6. Never share your UPI PIN, CVV number, or debit 
   card number with anyone under any circumstance.

7. Always verify the receiver's UPI ID carefully 
   before sending money to anyone.

8. Never scan random QR codes sent by strangers — 
   scanning can cause money to be withdrawn from 
   your account, not deposited.

=====================================================
💻 DEVICE SAFETY
=====================================================

9. Always keep your phone and laptop software and 
   apps updated to the latest version.

10. Never use public Wi-Fi for banking, UPI payments,
    or accessing sensitive personal accounts.

11. Lock your phone and laptop with a strong PIN 
    or password at all times.

12. Never install apps from unknown or unofficial 
    sources outside the Play Store or App Store.

=====================================================
🚨 REPORTING CHANNELS
=====================================================

If you are a victim of any cybercrime:

🌐 National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in
📞 Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (24x7, free to call)

Report immediately — the faster you report, the 
higher the chance of recovering lost money.

=====================================================
📌 REMEMBER
=====================================================
No bank, college, or government office will ever 
ask for your OTP, PIN, or password. 
If someone asks — it is ALWAYS a scam.

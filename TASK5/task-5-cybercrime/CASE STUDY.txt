=====================================================
TYPE OF CYBERCRIME: Phishing
=====================================================

WHAT IS PHISHING?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers send fake emails 
or messages pretending to be a trusted source such as a 
bank, college, or government office to trick victims into 
revealing personal information like passwords or OTPs.

=====================================================
HOW IT TYPICALLY HAPPENS (Step-by-Step)
=====================================================

Step 1: The attacker creates a fake email that looks 
        exactly like it is from a real bank or college 
        official.

Step 2: The email contains an urgent message such as 
        "Your account will be blocked. Click here 
        immediately to verify your details."

Step 3: The victim panics and clicks the link, which 
        opens a fake website that looks identical 
        to the real one.

Step 4: The victim enters their login ID, password, 
        or OTP on the fake website.

Step 5: The attacker captures this information and 
        gains unauthorized access to the victim's 
        bank account or college portal.

Step 6: Money is transferred or personal data is 
        stolen within minutes.

=====================================================
WHO IS TYPICALLY TARGETED?
=====================================================

- College students receiving fake scholarship emails
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with online security
- Job seekers receiving fake offer letters
- Anyone who receives urgent payment related messages

=====================================================
CONSEQUENCES
=====================================================

- Financial loss — money stolen from bank accounts
- Identity theft — personal data misused
- Loss of access to important accounts
- Mental stress and emotional distress
- Difficulty recovering stolen money or data

=====================================================
REAL SCENARIO EXAMPLE
=====================================================

A first year engineering student received an email 
claiming to be from his university stating that his 
fee payment had failed and he must re-enter his 
payment details within 24 hours or his admission 
would be cancelled. In panic, he clicked the link 
and entered his debit card details. Within minutes, 
Rs. 15,000 was withdrawn from his account. The email 
was fake — his university never sends such emails.
